1. Why is visualization effective?
    • Numbers are meaningless without context, and humans have a highly developed sense of vision. When given some numerical relation, we have to relate it to something we know for it to make sense. For example, if I'm told that the President has a 55% approval rating, my primary association is "Oh, that's a bit more than half," because I know what a half is. Visualizations skip the middle step. They are able to take numbers and display them in a form that a human can instantly interpret by pattern recognition and comparison. Without a visualization, finding patterns from numbers is more difficult, and we're more likely to make errors or draw inaccurate conclusions.

    • There are multiple tradeoffs that Cairo wants us to consider.
